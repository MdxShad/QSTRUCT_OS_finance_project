# Product Thesis

The structured products market in Asia is dominated by autocallable and snowball notes distributed through banks and broker networks.  Clients often specify the tenor, currency, underlyings, barrier level and coupon in a free‑form request for quote (RFQ).  Dealers then respond with a price or decline, based on internal risk appetite, hedge capacity and commercial objectives.

**Q‑Struct OS** is a research prototype that asks a provocative question: *what if a quoting desk could explore minor adjustments to an incoming RFQ that improve the probability of execution while respecting desk economics, risk limits and documentation constraints?*  Instead of accepting a quote as fixed, the system evaluates small changes to the barrier level, underlyings, coupon range or observation schedule and presents a ranked list of alternatives.

The product thesis is not to automate trading or pricing, but to offer a decision‑support tool that helps sales and structuring teams think creatively about how to win business without compromising risk and profitability.  All results are synthetic, derived from heuristic scoring functions and synthetic data.
